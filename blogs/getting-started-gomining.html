Getting Started with GoMining: Passive Bitcoin Income Without the Hardware

How I built a 1,010 TH/s mining fleet without owning a single ASIC — and what I learned about passive BTC income along the way.

What is GoMining?

GoMining is a platform that lets you participate in Bitcoin mining without purchasing, hosting, or maintaining physical ASIC hardware. Instead, you buy NFTs that represent real mining machines operating in professional data centres. The platform handles power, cooling, maintenance, and pool management — you just collect BTC daily.

I discovered it about two years ago while looking for ways to get BTC exposure beyond simply buying and holding. The idea of receiving daily BTC regardless of price action appealed to me as someone with a DevOps mindset — I liked the idea of infrastructure working for me while I sleep.

How Does It Actually Work?

Each miner NFT has two key specs: hashrate (TH/s) and energy efficiency (J/TH). Hashrate determines your share of the daily Bitcoin block rewards. Efficiency determines how much of that reward is consumed by the maintenance fee — the platform's way of accounting for electricity costs.

The daily reward formula is roughly:

Daily BTC ≈ (Your TH/s ÷ Network TH/s) × Block Rewards × (1 − Maintenance Fee %)

The better your efficiency, the lower your maintenance fee, and the more BTC you keep. This is why efficiency matters more than raw hashrate as you scale up.

Is It Worth It?

That depends entirely on your BTC price assumptions and time horizon. During bull markets, the BTC you earn compounds in fiat value. During bear markets, you're still accumulating sats at a steady rate. For me, it functions like a BTC dollar-cost averaging engine that also gives me daily liquidity — something pure HODLing doesn't offer.

My honest take: it's not a get-rich-quick scheme. It's a long-term BTC accumulation strategy that suits a certain type of investor. If you're already bullish on Bitcoin and want exposure beyond a buy-and-hold position, GoMining is worth exploring.
